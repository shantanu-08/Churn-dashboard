Key Findings
Business insights from the analysis
1
Month-to-month contracts account for 89% of all churn. Converting even 20% to annual contracts could save ₹10L/year.
2
First 6 months is the critical retention window. Customers who survive past 12 months show <10% churn probability.
3
Fiber optic users churn despite paying more. Root cause: service quality complaints and poor support response time.
4
Customers with 3+ services (bundle) churn 4× less than single-service customers — upselling reduces churn.
5
Electronic billing + no paperless opt-in correlates with 38% higher churn — disengaged customers.

Retention Strategy Recommendations

data-driven proposals
🎯
30-60-90 Onboarding Program
Assign a customer success touchpoint at Day 7, Day 30, and Day 60 for all new subscribers. Proactively share usage tips and offer upgrade incentives during the critical first 90 days.
Est. impact: -18% early churn
📦
Bundle Incentive Campaign
Offer 15% discount to month-to-month, single-service customers who upgrade to a bundle. Target the 891 "no add-ons" medium-risk segment with personalised offers via SMS/email.
Est. impact: -22% segment churn
🔔
Churn Risk Alert System
Build a simple scoring model using tenure, contract type, and service count. Flag customers crossing a risk threshold and trigger an automated retention call or exclusive loyalty offer.
Est. impact: -15% overall churn
🛠️
Fiber Quality Task Force
Form a cross-functional team to investigate fiber optic complaints. Reduce average support response time from 48hrs to under 6hrs. Monitor NPS scores monthly for this segment.
Est. impact: -30% fiber churn
📅
Annual Contract Migration Drive
Run a quarterly campaign offering month-to-month customers 2 free months if they switch to annual billing. Frame as a price-lock guarantee against future increases.
Est. impact: ₹10L revenue saved
📊
Monthly Churn Review Meeting
Institutionalise a monthly BA-led review meeting sharing this dashboard with leadership. Track strategy KPIs and adjust campaigns based on real churn movement — making data culture central.
Est. impact: Ongoing optimisation
